My Manifesto!

I will take risks and not be afraid to go outside the box.

I will push myself to take more risks and not just stick to what feels safe. Thinking outside the box can lead to more unique and meaningful work, even if it doesn’t always turn out perfectly. I’m working on trusting my ideas more and being okay with trying things that might not work right away, and also accept that not everyone will understand or like my work.

I will be patient with myself not feel down if I am confused or lost.

I tend to get frustrated or feel behind when things don’t work for me right away, but being a design student has taught me that struggle is part of building skill. I don’t want to be too hard on myself when I don’t understand something immediately. Instead, I want to treat learning code as a slow process and give myself space to improve step by step.

I will learn to balance aesthetics with function, and not lose my personal design style in the work that I create

I will try to make work that is both functional and visually interesting. It’s important to me that what I create works well and is easy to understand, but I also want it to feel intentional and engaging. I also want to continue to have the things I create show my personality and design style in them, and not create boring, average work.

I will ask for help without hesitation and not struggle silently.

I will not let frustration build silently. If I don’t understand something, I will ask questions, look for clarification, and seek help when needed.

I will learn how to connect my previous design knowledge to code, and learn how intertwining them will help me in my future career.

I will use my existing design knowledge as a strength in this class. Even if I am new to coding, I already understand ideas like composition, visual balance, hierarchy, and creative problem solving. I will look for ways to connect visual thinking with logical thinking, and use design principles to help me understand how code works alongside design. I also want to use both design and coding skills together to open up more creative and professional opportunities for me in the future.
