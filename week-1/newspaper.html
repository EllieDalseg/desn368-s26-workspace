"NASA's Artemis II crew safely splashed down in the Pacific Ocean"

The Artemis II mission successfully completed its 10-day lunar flyby, with the Orion spacecraft splashing down safely in the Pacific Ocean off the coast of San Diego on April 10, 2026. The four-astronaut crew returned after breaking records for the farthest human journey into space, setting the stage for future lunar landings.

Mission Success and Next Steps:

  • Record Breaking: The mission was the first to take humans around the moon since 1972, traveling deeper into space than ever before.
  • Upcoming Missions: This success paves the way for the Artemis III and IV missions, planned for 2027 and beyond, which aim to land astronauts back on the lunar surface.
