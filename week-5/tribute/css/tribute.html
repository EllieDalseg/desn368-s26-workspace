HAYLEY WILLIAMS

A powerhouse vocalist and songwriter known for her vibrant energy, emotionally honest lyrics, and iconic role as the frontwoman of Paramore.

Early life and Beginnings

Hayley Williams was born on December 27, 1988, in Meridian, Mississippi, and spent her early childhood there before moving to Franklin, Tennessee as a teenager after her parents’ divorce. In Franklin during the early 2000s, she began seriously pursuing music, meeting future bandmates and getting involved in the local scene.

(2004-2007) Formation of Paramore & Breakthrough

By 2004, still a teenager, she had signed a record deal and co-founded Paramore. In 2005, the band released their debut album All We Know Is Falling, introducing them to the alternative scene, but their big breakthrough came in 2007 with Riot!. The success of songs like Misery Business made Williams one of the defining voices of pop-punk.

(2009-2012) Growth, Challenges & Mainstream Success

In 2009, they released Brand New Eyes, though the band went through major lineup changes around 2010. During this time, Williams also gained wider recognition through her collaboration with B.o.B on Airplanes, which became a global hit within days.

(2013-2017) Artistic Shift & Personal Openness

In 2013, Paramore released their self-titled album Paramore, marking a new creative direction, with Ain't It Fun becoming one of their biggest successes. By 2017, After Laughter showed another shift in sound, blending pop and new wave while Williams became more open about mental health and identity.

(2020–Present) Solo Career & Continued Evolution

In 2020, she released her solo debut Petals for Armor, followed by the more stripped-back Flowers for Vases / descansosin 2021. By 2023, Paramore returned with This Is Why, continuing their evolution and long-term influence in alternative music.
